FAQs

  1. Firefly Basics: Firefly 101

    1. What is Firefly?

      Firefly is a funding platform for creative productions. Everything from short films, TV Series to Movies. Firefly is full of ambitious, innovative, and imaginative productions that are brought to life through the direct support of people like you!

      Since our launch on January 13, 2016, over $2 billion(wishful thinking..) has been pledged by more than 10 million people, funding more than 97,000 creative productions. If you like stats, there's lots more here.

    2. What are the basics?

      A production is a finite work with a clear goal that you’d like to bring to life.

      The funding goal is the amount of money that a creator needs to complete their production.

      Funding on Firefly is all-or-nothing. No one will be charged for a pledge towards a production unless it reaches its funding goal. This way, creators always have the budget they scoped out before moving forward.

      A creator is the person or team behind the production idea, working to bring it to life.

      Backers are folks who pledge money to join creators in bringing productions to life.

      Rewards are a creator's chance to share a piece of their production with their backer community. Typically, these are one-of-a-kind experiences, limited editions, or copies of the creative work being produced.

    3. How does Firefly work?

      Thousands of productions are funding on Firefly at any given moment. Each production is independently created and crafted by the person behind it. The filmmakers, musicians, artists, and designers you see on Firefly have complete control and responsibility over their productions. They spend weeks building their production pages, shooting their videos, and brainstorming what rewards to offer backers. When they feel ready, a creator can launch their production on the site and share it with their community.

      Every production creator sets their production's funding goal and deadline. If people like the production, they can pledge money to make it happen. If the production succeeds in reaching its funding goal, all backers' credit cards are charged when time expires. If the production falls short, no one is charged. Funding on Firefly is all-or-nothing.

    4. What is Firefly for?

      Everything on Firefly must be a production. A production has a clear goal, like making a short film, a movie, or TV episodes. A production will eventually be completed, and something will be produced by it.

      Firefly does not allow productions to fundraise for charity or offer financial incentives.

    5. Why is funding all-or-nothing?

      All-or-nothing funding is a core part of Firefly and it has a number of advantages:

      It's less risk for everyone. If you need $5,000, it's tough having $1,000 and a bunch of people expecting you to complete a $5,000 production. 

      It motivates. If people want to see a production come to life, they're going to spread the word.   

      It works. Of the productions that have reached 20% of their funding goal, 81% were successfully funded. Of the productions that have reached 60% of their funding goal, 98% were successfully funded. productions either make their goal or find little support. There's little in-between.

    6. Where do productions come from?

      productions on Firefly come from fans, producers, film makers and creative people all over. They may be modest or ambitious, new ideas, or classic ideas with a twist. Every production is unique and is something that someone wants to exist in the world.

      If you’ve got a creative idea you think is perfect for Firefly.

    7. Why do people back productions?

      Many backers are rallying around their friends' productions. Some are supporting a new effort from someone they've long admired. Some are just inspired by a new idea, while others are motivated to pledge by a production's rewards — a copy of what's being produced, a limited edition, or a custom experience related to the production.

      Backing a production is more than just pledging funds to a creator. It's pledging your support to a creative idea that you want to see exist in the world.

    8. Where do backers come from?

      The majority of initial funding usually comes from the fans and friends of each production. If they like it, they'll spread the word to their friends, and so on. Press, blogs, Twitter, Facebook, and Firefly itself are also big sources of traffic and pledges. Altogether, millions of people visit Firefly every week.

    9. What do backers get in return?

      Backers that support a production on Firefly get an inside look at the creative process, and help that production come to life. They also get to choose from a variety of unique rewards offered by the production creator. Rewards vary from production to production, but often include votes for selections of casts or an experience unique to the production.

      production creators keep 100% ownership of their work, and Firefly cannot be used to offer equity, financial returns, or to solicit loans.

    10. What are the fees?

      If a production is successfully funded, Firefly applies a 5% fee to the funds collected. Our payments processor will also apply payment processing fees (roughly 3-5%). The complete fee breakdowns are available.

      If funding isn't successful, there are no fees.

    11. Who is Firefly?

      We are an independent company of 4 people based in Redmond, WA. We spend our time improving the site, answering questions from backers and creators, and finding great new productions to share with you. Every day is an adventure — we get to experience productions as they happen!

  2. Firefly Basics: Accountability

    1. Who is responsible for completing a production as promised?

      It's the production creator's responsibility to complete their production. Firefly is not involved in the development of the productions themselves.

      Firefly does not guarantee productions or investigate a creator's ability to complete their production. On Firefly, backers (you!) ultimately decide the validity and worthiness of a production by whether they decide to fund it. 

    2. How do backers know if a production will follow through?

      Launching a Firefly is a very public act, and creators put their reputations at risk when they do.

      Backers should look for creators who share a clear plan for how their production will be completed, and who have a history of bringing their creative ventures and other productions to fruition. Creators are encouraged to share links and as much background information as possible so backers can make informed decisions about the productions they support.

      If a creator has no demonstrable experience in doing something like their production or doesn't share key information, backers should take that into consideration. Does the creator include links to any websites that show work related to the production, or past productions? Does the creator appear in the video? Have they connected via Facebook?

      Don't hesitate to request information from a creator. You can always reach out before pledging via the "Contact me" button on the production page.

    3. How do I know a production creator is who they claim they are?

      Perhaps you are friends with the creator, or you heard about the production from a trusted source.

      Looking for more information? Read through the description and watch the video to get a better sense of the person or team behind the production.

      In the creator bio section of each production, you can find additional resources, like links to websites, relevant background information, and in some cases, a connected Facebook account. For productions that have launched since May 19, 2014, you'll also find a verified name with a check mark next to it. This person is the creator of the production or a part of the company or team behind it, and they verified their identity through an automated process. 

    4. What should creators do if they're having problems completing their production?

      If problems come up, creators are expected to post a production update explaining the situation. Sharing the story, speed bumps and all, is crucial. Most backers support productions because they want to see something happen and they'd like to be a part of it. Creators who are honest and transparent will usually find backers to be understanding.

      It's not uncommon for things to take longer than expected. Sometimes the execution of the production proves more difficult than the creator had anticipated. If a creator is making a good faith effort to complete their production and is transparent about it, backers should do their best to be patient and understanding while demanding continued accountability from the creator.

      If the problems are severe enough that the creator can't fulfill their production, creators need to find a resolution. Steps should include offering refunds, detailing exactly how funds were used, and other actions to satisfy backers.

    5. What is a creator obligated to do once their production is funded?

      When a production is successfully funded, the creator is responsible for completing the production and fulfilling each reward. Their fundamental obligation to backers is to finish all the work that was promised. Once a creator has done so, they’ve fulfilled their obligation to their backers. At the same time, backers must understand that Firefly is not a store. When you back a production, you’re helping to create something new — not ordering something that already exists. There’s a chance something could happen that prevents the creator from being able to finish the production as promised. If a creator is absolutely unable to complete the production and fulfill rewards, they must make every reasonable effort to find another way of bringing the production to a satisfying conclusion for their backers.

    6. Can Firefly refund the money if a production is unable to fulfill?

      No, Firefly doesn't issue refunds. Transactions are between backers and creators directly. Creators receive all funds (less fees) soon after their campaign ends.

    7. Why can't Firefly guarantee productions?

      We started Firefly as a new way for creators and audiences to work together to make things. Many traditional funding systems are risk-averse and profit-focused, and tons of great ideas never get a chance. Firefly opens the door to a world full of creative possibilities, one where anyone can help decide which bright idea will become tomorrow's reality.

      Firefly is full of ambitious, innovative, and imaginative ideas. Many of the productions you see on Firefly are in earlier stages of development and are looking for a community to bring them to life. The fact that Firefly allows creators to take risks and attempt to create new things is a feature, not a bug.

    8. What does Firefly do to protect its community?

      We have a dedicated Integrity team that monitors the system for suspicious activity. This team screens reports that are sent to us by our community and they take action if they find something that does not align with our rules.

      It's important to remember that Firefly is built on an all-or-nothing funding system. No one is charged until a production meets its funding goal and the funding period ends. This gives backers time to fully evaluate a production, and it also gives our Integrity team some time to look into any concerns raised by backers.


      For more information about how Firefly works, visit our Trust & Safety page.

  3. Firefly Basics: Getting involved

    1. How do I start a production?

      Anyone that meets our creator requirements is eligible to launch a production on Firefly.

      Just head to our start page to begin building your production. You can find tips on structuring and running your production in our Creator Handbook, and refer to our rules to make sure your production idea is a good fit. Meanwhile, feel free to save your production as a draft and return to finish it up or submit it for review at a later date.

    2. I'm interested in partnering with Firefly. How can I get in touch?

      Great! If you're an organization, institution, or entity interested in partnering with us, please reach out to us at partnerships@Firefly.com.

      Apologies for any inconvenience, but we're not undertaking any sponsorship opportunities at this time.

    3. Does Firefly have a responsible security disclosure procedure?

      We sure do. If you’ve discovered a potential bug or vulnerability with Firefly.com, please contact us here and we'll help make sure the information gets shared with our engineering team.

  4. Firefly Basics: Reporting a production

    1. How can I report something to Firefly?

      Every production page has a "Report this production to Firefly" button at the bottom of it. You can use this option to notify us about a violation of policies).

      If you receive any messages that appear to be spam our outside of our guidelines, please mark them as spam to flag them for our team. Creators can also mark comments as spam.

      While we're not able to respond to each individual report, we do have a dedicated team that investigates every report that we receive. We appreciate you bringing anything out of the ordinary to our attention!

    2. How do I report a copyright violation?

      If you're not sure whether material on Firefly infringes your copyright, please talk with a lawyer before filing a complaint with us. Misrepresenting that material infringes your copyright may subject you to liability for damages, including attorneys' fees and costs incurred by production creators or other parties.

      If you've spoken with a lawyer and think your material is being infringed, try contacting the production creator directly using the "Contact me" button on the production page. If that doesn't work, you can submit a complaint using.

    3. How do I report a trademark violation?

      If you believe a Firefly production is infringing on a trademark you hold, you can report a trademark violation by emailing copyright@Firefly.com. We'll evaluate and respond to every report we receive.
